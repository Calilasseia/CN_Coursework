The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Large Emerald (Geometra papilionaria)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5490 8351 (VC 58), on 2020/07/04


One of several moths with "Emerald" in the vernacular name, and the largest thereof, as the name suggests. This species may be found in numbers where its larval foodplant, Birch, occurs, and is one of the more spectacular summer visitors to moth traps. It shares with the other "Emerald" moths, a tendency to fade in colour fairly quickly after emerging from the pupa, and the colour can fade to almost beige after two or three weeks of adult life, so freshly emerged specimens are the premium choice for the photographer aiming to display this species at its finest.
