The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Ichneumon Wasp

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5480 8353 (VC 58), on 2021/05/29


This ichneumon wasp is awaiting identification, and as an illustration of the scale of the problem these insects pose, there are no less than THREE THOUSAND species of ichenumon wasp in the UK fauna! These insects lay their eggs inside the larvae of other insects, and the wasp larvae consume their host from the inside out as they develop. Numerous species are kleptoparasites of Lepidoptera larvae, while others choose beetle larvae as their hosts, and some are specialists on Diptera (true flies). Note the ovipositor at the rear of the abdomen of this specimen, which identifies it as a female.
