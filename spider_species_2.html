The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Unidenitied Linyphiid Spider

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5479 8353, on 2016 06 24


This spider is still awaiting precise identification, though it is probably a member of the Genus Tenuiphantes. Spiders in the Family Linyphiidae are generally small (many are referred to colloquially as "money spiders"), and in the case of members of some Genera in this Family, are notable for having remarkable modifications of the cephalothorax in males. The canonical example of this is Walckenaeria acuminata, whose males have a bizarre modification of the cephalothorax, in which the eyes are placed upon a strange curved "tower" rising above the rest of the cephalothorax.
