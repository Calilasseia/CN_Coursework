The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Elephant Hawk Moth (Deilephila elpenor)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5480 8353 (VC 58), on 2020/06/20


This large, imposing and wonderfully coloured species not only boasts an impressive appearance as an adult, but the larva is also notable for being large, and resembling an elephant's trunk in appearance, though no elephant has large eye spot markings on its trunk, as seen upon the larva of this species! The larva can inflate its forward body segments to enlarge the eye spots, giving the larva a resemblance tio a small snake - an example of fright colouration. Larvae feed upon various Willowherbs, though they have in recent years been observed feeding upon cultivated Fuchsias in addition to their traditional choice of Willowherbs.
