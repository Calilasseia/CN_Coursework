The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Biorhiza pallida

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5490 8351 (VC 58), on 2020/07/23


This is a particularly unusual insect, which took me a week to identify upon account of it resembling nothing I'd seen before! This is Biorhiza pallida, a gall wasp that alternates between completely different adult generations. The first generation consists of wasps that look like typical small wasps, and which are divided into male and female individuals, mating sexually, and laying their eggs in Oak leaves, The larvae then feed within the galls produced, and produce a second generation of adults, which by contrast with the first generation, are all female, wingless, and reproduce parthenogenetically! The individual depicted above is a member of that second, wingless and parthenogenetic generation.
