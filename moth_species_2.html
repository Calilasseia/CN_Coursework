The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Scarce Silver-Lines (Bena bicolorana)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5490 8351 (VC 58), on 2020/07/04


While the name of this species indicates rarity to the untrained observer, this moth is actually fairly widespread in the Uk, though it has a patchy distribution, and is less common than the related Green Silver-Lines. Larval foodplant of this species is Oak, and the species may be found in undisturbed mature deciduous woodlands with a significant proportion of mature Oak trees. several of these have been observed at Norton Priory over the past decade, with the above specimen being a recent addition to the observation database.
