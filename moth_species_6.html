The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Poplar Hawk Moth (Laothoe populi)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5490 8351 (VC 58), on 2020/07/23


Among the largest of the British moths, this species rests in a manner resembling a collection of dead leaves, and is superbly camouflaged when adopting its typical resting posture. Like numerous other hawk moths, this species can be remarkably tame, and is easily persuaded to rest upon one's hand to pose for photographs of a somewhat different nature from the usual biological recording photos! As the name suggests, the larval foodplants are Black Poplar and White Poplar, though the lage and imposing larvae wull also feed upon the leaves of Aspen and various Sallows.
