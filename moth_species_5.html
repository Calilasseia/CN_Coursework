The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Brimstone Moth (Opisthograptis luteolata)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5490 8351 (VC 58), on 2020/07/23


One of the "catwalk stars" of the British moth fauna, with its rich sulphur-yellow colour making it stand out against its more sombre hued cousins. Another species whose larvae resemble twigs in appearance The favoured larval foodplants of this species include Hawthorn and Blackthorn. Can be somewhat nervous and skittish around the light trap at first, but sometimes settles to provide the photographer with superlative portrait poses!
