The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Swallowtailed Moth (Ourapteryx sambucaria)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5490 8351 (VC 58), on 2020/07/04


A familiar sight at night in deciduous woodlands with open clearings, and possessing a ghostly appearance when flying around a light source, such as the mercury vapour bulbs used for moth trapping. One of the more exotic-looking members of the UK moth fauna, this species is surprising common, and can be enticed to present itself in any location where its larval foodplant, Ivy, is present in sufficient abundance. This is one of the numerous UK moth species whose larvae resemble twigs in appearance - the larvae are superbly camouflaged when resting on stems of the foodplant.
