The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Cockchafer Beetle (Melonontha melonontha)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5480 8353 (VC 58), on 2021/05/29


Cockchafer beetles are familiar sight to moth trappers during May and June, when the adults are active and seeking mates. One of the unusual features of this species, is that the number of "fingers" on the antennae are diagnostic - females have six, males have seven. These insects are also notable for being a source of amusing mayhem around a light trap, and they can actually constitute a hazard to exposed light bulbs, courtesy of the fact that these beetles are heavily armoured, and have a habit of trying to crash into the light bulb!
