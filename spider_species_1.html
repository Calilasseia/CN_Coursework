The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Cucumber Spider (Araniella cf. cucurbitina)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5479 8353, on 2016 06 24


This spider is one of five very similar species in the Genus Araniella, hence the designation "cf. cucurbitina". The two commonest species are Araniella cucurbitina itself, and the very similar Araniella opisthographa, which are usually difficult to separate visually - for a definitive identification, detailed examination of the male palp or the female epigyne are required. Three other species found in the UK fauna are Araniella inconspicua, Araniella displicata and Araniella alpica, but these three speices have very restricted distributions, and are unlikely to behind found in the Cheshire area in the foreseeable future.
