The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Long-Jawed Orb Weaver (Tetragnatha sp.)

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5480 8353 (VC 58), on 2021/05/29


There are several members of the Genus Tetragnatha resident in the UK, all of which are difficult to separate to species level, and all of which require genital dissection for a definitive ID (either the male palp or the female epigyne). Members of this Genus can be encountered in suburban gardens as well as in wild habitats, and these spiders occupy a range of habitats such as deciduous and coniferous woodlands, grasslands, heaths, bogs, and coastal habitats excluding bare sand dunes.
