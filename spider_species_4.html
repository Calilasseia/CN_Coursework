The Invertebrate Fauna Of Norton Priory



Theridion pictum.

Specimen observed & photographed at grid reference SJ 5480 8353 (VC 58), on 2021/05/29


This is one of the many UK spiders that lack a common or vernacular name, and only has a scientific name. It prefers moist habitats, such as wetlands, deciduous woodland receiving frequent rainfall, and has a patchy distribution in England, with its main strongholds in the south and midlands, though a cluster of records centred upon the south of the River Mersey into North Wales exists in the biodiversity database, with its local stronghold being positioned between the River Mersey at Runcorn, and the River Dee through Chester.
